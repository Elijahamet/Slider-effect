A moose is the largest member of the deer family, known for its towering height and broad antlers. Found in North America, Europe, and Asia, moose are solitary herbivores that thrive in forests and wetlands. They are strong swimmers and primarily feed on shrubs, trees, and aquatic plants. Breeds/Subspecies: - North American Moose (Alces alces americanus) - Eurasian Moose (Alces alces alces) - Shiras Moose (Alces alces shirasi)

A parrot is a colorful, intelligent bird known for its ability to mimic sounds and speech. Found in tropical and subtropical regions, parrots have strong beaks, zygodactyl feet, and vibrant plumage. They are omnivorous, feeding on seeds, fruits, nuts, and sometimes insects. Breeds/Subspecies: - Macaw (Ara species) - African Grey Parrot (Psittacus erithacus) - Cockatoo (Cacatua species) - Budgerigar (Melopsittacus undulatus) - Amazon Parrot (Amazona species)

ANIMAL
An elephant is the largest land mammal, known for its massive size, long trunk, and large ears. Elephants are intelligent and social animals, living in herds and found primarily in Africa and Asia. They are herbivores, feeding on grasses, fruits, and bark. Breeds/Subspecies: - African Bush Elephant (Loxodonta africana) - African Forest Elephant (Loxodonta cyclotis) - Asian Elephant (Elephas maximus)

A tiger is a powerful and solitary carnivore, known for its striking orange coat with black stripes. Tigers are found in Asia and are skilled hunters, preying on large animals like deer and wild boar. They are excellent swimmers and typically live in forests, grasslands, and swamps. **Breeds/Subspecies: - Bengal Tiger (Panthera tigris tigris) - Siberian Tiger (Panthera tigris altaica) - Indochinese Tiger (Panthera tigris corbetti) - Malayan Tiger (Panthera tigris jacksoni) - Sumatran Tiger (Panthera tigris sumatrae)

An owl is a nocturnal bird of prey, known for its round face, large eyes, and silent flight. Owls are skilled hunters, using their excellent hearing and vision to catch small mammals, birds, and insects. They are found in various habitats around the world, from forests to deserts. Breeds/Subspecies: - Barn Owl (Tyto alba) - Snowy Owl (Bubo scandiacus) - Great Horned Owl (Bubo virginianus) - Eastern Screech Owl (Megascops asio) - Barred Owl (Strix varia)

ANIMAL
Squirrels are small, agile rodents, with three main types: tree squirrels, ground squirrels, and flying squirrels. Tree squirrels, like the Eastern Gray, Red, and Fox squirrels, are known for their climbing skills. Ground squirrels, such as the California Ground and Rock squirrels, live in burrows and are commonly found in open areas. Flying squirrels, including the Northern and Southern Flying squirrels, glide through the air using flaps of skin. Each breed adapts to its environment, playing key roles in ecosystems.

Bears are large mammals found across various habitats worldwide, with eight distinct species. The most well-known are the Brown Bear (including the Grizzly), which is found in North America, and the Polar Bear, adapted to the Arctic regions. The American Black Bear is widespread in forests, while the Asiatic Black Bear is found in Asia. Other species include the Andean Bear, the Sloth Bear, and the Giant Panda, native to China. Each species of bear has unique traits and adaptations suited to its environment, from the Polar Bear’s thick fur to the Panda’s bamboo diet.

Lions are large carnivorous mammals known for their majestic appearance and social behavior. There are two main subspecies: the African Lion, found in savannas, and the Asiatic Lion, which resides in India's Gir Forest. Lions live in prides, working together to hunt and protect their territory. Their powerful build and striking mane make them iconic animals.

Zebras are herbivorous mammals known for their black-and-white striped coats. There are three species: the Plains Zebra, the Grevy's Zebra, and the Mountain Zebra. Plains Zebras are the most common and live in grasslands, while Grevy's Zebras are found in East Africa’s semi-arid regions. Mountain Zebras inhabit more rugged areas. Each species has distinct features but shares social behavior, living in groups for protection.
